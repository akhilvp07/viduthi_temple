Ulakudaya Perumal Temple

A Sacred Legacy of Devotion and History

The Sacred Origins

The Ulakudaya Perumal Temple, associated with the 'Kuranganthottam' family and the 'Cheruvazha Channanmar', has its roots near the 'Kunnathumala Temple'. This sacred site, known as the moola sthanam, lies west of the 'Chengannur Devi Temple' and close to the 'Thiruvandoor' road.

Historical Journey

Ancient Kingdoms

The region was divided between Madhura Rajyam and Vaiga Rajyam, leading to frequent conflicts between the two kingdoms.

The Great Battle

During a significant battle, the Madhura Raja caused extensive damage to the temple at the moola sthanam.

Exile and New Beginnings

Ulakudaya Perumal and his family established new temples across various locations including Chavara, Mullaramkodu, and others.

Vakkom Daivapura

The tragic events at Vakkom Daivapura led to the establishment of the Bhadrakali Temple.

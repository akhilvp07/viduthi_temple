About our temple

The Sacred Origins

The Ulakudaya Perumal Temple, associated with the 'Kuranganthottam' family and the 'Cheruvazha Channanmar', has its roots near the 'Kunnathumala Temple', west of the 'Chengannur Devi Temple' and close to the 'Thiruvandoor' road. This original location is considered its moola sthanam. The Bhadrakali Temple and 'Nagadeva Sthanam' at this site are part of a very ancient family lineage.

Temple Lineage

Formation of Kingdoms and Temple Destruction

In ancient times, the region was divided into two kingdoms: Madhura Rajyam and Vaiga Rajyam. Madhura Rajyam was ruled by the Madhura Raja, a devotee of Shiva. Vaiga Rajyam was ruled by Ulakudaya Perumal, who worshipped Bhadrakali. Frequent battles took place between these kingdoms. During one such battle, Ulakudaya Perumal was defeated. The Madhura Raja, riding an elephant, caused extensive damage to the temple at the 'moola sthanam', including the Devi Temple's walls.

Exile and Temple Foundations

After his defeat, Ulakudaya Perumal, along with his sister and five brothers (Thampurans), was exiled. As they journeyed, they settled in various locations where temples were subsequently established in their honor. These temples were built at:

  • Chavara
  • Mullaramkodu
  • Pongummoodu
  • Venjaramoodu
  • Mandapakkunnu
  • Vakkom Daivapura

    • Tragedy at Vakkom Daivapura

    At Vakkom Daivapura, a tragic event unfolded where the five 'Thampurans' were killed in the presence of the Maraykkanmar. Ulakudaya Perumal and his sister fled further, crossing Vakkom Irangukadavu, and finally rested at Kallinmoodu Kadappuram. Here, Ulakudaya Perumal's sister married a young man named Ulakan, and their descendants became known as the Kuranganthottam family.Ulakudaya Perumal, having escaped further conflict with the 'Kadal Maraykkanmar', is said to have thrust his sword into the ground at a specific spot and died. His sister's son later built a temple dedicated to Bhadrakali at this very location, installing her idol.

    Relocation of the Temple

    Later, during the reign of Marthanda Varma Maharaja, when the Varkala canal was being constructed, this temple became an obstacle. As a result, the temple was relocated to 'Viduthi Vilakam'. The new temple retained some exquisitely crafted sections from the original structure, including a beautifully carved 'peedam'. From this time onwards, a festival known as the 'Bhadrakali Pooja' and 'Rappada' has been celebrated. This festival was traditionally led by the Karnavar (elders/heads of the family): Thiruvan, Ulakan, and Kochu Shankaran Asan, a relative of Sree Narayana Guru.

    Associated Temples and Practices

    The main temples associated with this lineage include:

    • Valiya Melathil Ayani Vilakam Devi Temple
    • Muthana Panayil Temple
    • Perettil Padinjattathil Temple
    • Kollam Vilakam
    • Thettikulam Madan Kavu
    • Pullanikkodu Poykayil
    • Pillaveedu Annapoorneswari Temple
    • Karunilakkodu Kuzhivilakom Temple

    A unique aspect of these temples is that although they are all connected to Ulakudaya Perumal, only the Viduthi Vilakam Temple has a separate shrine dedicated specifically to him.

    Connection to Sree Narayana Guru

    The temple at 'Viduthi Vilakam' was frequently visited by Sree Narayana Guru. His family members, including his father Madan Asan, his younger brothers Kochandi Vaidyan and Kuttiperumal Vaidyan, and their sister Kali, were also associated with this place.

    The serene environment of the temple made it a suitable retreat for meditation and spiritual practice. Sree Narayana Guru is believed to have conducted various discourses here, inspiring many with his teachings. This site became a hub for social and religious reform under his influence, leaving a lasting legacy. The historical importance of this place continues to attract devotees and scholars alike.

    Spiritual Traditions

    The temple complex features an ancient 'sarppakavu' and shrines for sub-deities such as Yogeeswaran, Brahmarakshas, and Manthramoorthy. Inside the 'Chuttambalam', there are shrines for Perumal(Shiva) and Ganapathi.

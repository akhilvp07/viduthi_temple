About Our Temple

The Ulakudaya Perumal Temple and Its Lineage

The Ulakudaya Perumal Temple, also known in connection with the 'Kuranganthottam' family and the 'Cheruvazha Channanmar', traces its origins to a site near the 'Kunnathumala Temple', west of the 'Chengannur Devi Temple' and close to the 'Thiruvandoor' road. This original location is considered its moola sthanam (primary or root location). The Bhadrakali Temple and 'Nagadeva Sthanam' (abode of the Snake God) at this site are part of a very ancient family lineage.

Historical Context and Conflict

In ancient times, the region was divided into two kingdoms: Madhura Rajyam and Vaiga Rajyam. Madhura Rajyam was ruled by the Madhura Raja (King), while Vaiga Rajyam was under the rule of Ulakudaya Perumal. Frequent battles occurred between these two kingdoms. The Madhura Raja worshipped Shiva, while Ulakudaya Perumal's deity was Bhadrakali. During one such battle, Ulakudaya Perumal suffered defeat. The Madhura Raja, riding an elephant, caused significant damage to the temple at the moola sthanam, including the walls of the Devi Temple.

Exile and Establishment of New Temples

Following this defeat, Ulakudaya Perumal and his lineage, including his sister and five brothers (Thampurans/Lords), were exiled. As they traveled and settled in different locations, temples were established in their honor. The primary locations where these temples were built include:

  • Chavara
  • Mullaramkodu
  • Pongummoodu
  • Venjaramoodu
  • Mandapakkunnu
  • Vakkom Daivapura

The Story at Vakkom Daivapura

At Vakkom Daivapura, it is said that the five 'Thampurans' (brothers) were killed in the presence of the 'Maraykkanmar'. Ulakudaya Perumal and his sister then traveled further, crossing the 'Vakkom Irangukadavu', eventually resting at 'Kallinmoodu Kadappuram'. There, a young man named Ulakan married Ulakudaya Perumal's sister, and their descendants became known as the 'Kuranganthottam' family.

Ulakudaya Perumal, having escaped further conflict with the 'Kadal Maraykkanmar', is said to have thrust his sword into the ground at a specific spot and died. His sister's son later built a temple dedicated to Bhadrakali at this very location, installing her idol.

Relocation and Later Developments

Later, during the reign of Marthanda Varma Maharaja, when the Varkala canal was being constructed, this temple became an obstacle. As a result, the temple was relocated to 'Viduthi Vilakam'. The new temple retained some exquisitely crafted sections from the original structure, including a beautifully carved 'peedam'.

From this time onwards, a festival known as the 'Bhadrakali Pooja' and 'Rappada' has been celebrated. This festival was traditionally led by the Karnavar (elders/heads of the family): Thiruvan, Ulakan, and Kochu Shankaran Asan, a relative of Sree Narayana Guru.

Associated Temples and Practices

The main temples associated with this lineage include:

  • Valiya Melathil Ayani Vilakam Devi Temple
  • Muthana Panayil Temple
  • Perettil Padinjattathil Temple
  • Kollam Vilakam
  • Thettikulam Madan Kavu
  • Pullanikkodu Poykayil
  • Pillaveedu Annapoorneswari Temple
  • Karunilakkodu Kuzhivilakom Temple

A unique aspect of these temples is that although they are all connected to Ulakudaya Perumal, only the Viduthi Vilakam Temple has a separate shrine dedicated specifically to him.

Connection to Sree Narayana Guru and Temple Practices

The temple at 'Viduthi Vilakam' was frequently visited by Sree Narayana Guru. His family members, including his father Madan Asan, his younger brothers Kochandi Vaidyan and Kuttiperumal Vaidyan, and their sister Kali, were also associated with this place.

The temple complex includes an ancient serpent grove (Sarppakavu) and shrines for sub-deities such as Yogeeswaran, Brahmarakshas, and Manthramoorthy. The temple, after a period of disrepair, was restored 13 years ago and now follows a Shatadhara Prathishta. Within the 'Chuttambalam', there are shrines for Perumal (Shiva) and Ganapathi. The temple faces west, in reverence to Ganga Devi. According to the temple astrologer, rituals have been performed to remove 'Marutha', 'Brahma Pretham', 'Chathan', 'Pey', and 'Arukola' as per Tantric traditions.

The Ayilya Puja is performed regularly, and the Uruli Kamizhthal is a significant offering. During festivals, Thottam Pattu is performed first, followed by Kalam Pooja, Thampuran Pattu, and Padappurappadu.

The temple is situated in a beautiful natural setting and is considered a place where devotees' prayers are answered. All temple customs are now conducted according to Tantric practices, whereas in the past, they were overseen by family elders.
